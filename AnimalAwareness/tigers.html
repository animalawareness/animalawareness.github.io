TIGERS

Tigers, the biggest cat species, formerly roamed a broad area of territory stretching from Turkey through South and Southeast Asia to the far eastern coast of the continent. They are now only found in a few isolated locations in Russia's far east, China, and South and Southeast Asia. Tigers are primarily nocturnal, reclusive animals who only interact with one another during mating season. Interestingly, tigers are excellent swimmers and have even been seen to hunt in water despite their enormous size and amazing ability to hunt on land.

Tigers are in grave danger despite their beauty and legendary reputation. Tigers have been ruthlessly killed by poachers across their habitat, either driving them to extinction or making them vulnerable to extinction, depending on the species.

There were nine different subspecies of tigers at one time, but in the past 80 years, three of them have vanished entirely. All six of the existing tiger species are regarded as either critically endangered or endangered. The Bengal tiger, the most abundant of the subspecies, has just around 2,500 individuals left in the wild.

It has been projected that all tigers may go extinct in the wild over the next ten years if we don't take prompt action to conserve and safeguard the surviving population. That is one of the main justifications for why it is necessary to adopt strict anti-wildlife trafficking legislation and tiger safeguards.
