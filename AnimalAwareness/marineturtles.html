MARINE TURTLES

Some of the oldest living things on Earth are marine turtles. The smallest turtles weigh between 85 and 100 pounds, while the largest, the leatherback turtle, weighs up to 2,000 pounds. Threats to marine turtles of all species exist in our oceans.

In all warm and temperate oceans around the world, there are seven species of marine turtles. As air-breathing reptiles designed for a life in the ocean, these turtles can swim large distances at great depths thanks to their aerodynamic shape and strong flippers.

Every year, these amazing birds travel hundreds of miles to their eating and nesting regions. After their initial excursion after hatching, the majority of marine turtles rarely, if ever, come back to the shore.

Three of the seven species of sea turtles, the leatherback, hawksbill, and Kemps' Ridley, are severely endangered. Tens of thousands of these animals are killed each year to meet the demand from black markets for their eggs, meat, skin, and shells.

Marine turtles can continue to swim in our oceans for millions of years with the help of strict regulation and enforcement.
