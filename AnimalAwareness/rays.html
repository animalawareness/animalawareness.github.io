RAYS

The majestic and enigmatic ray, a shark relative, is distinguished by its bat-like body shape and long, slender tail. The two main groups of rays, oceanic (Giant) and reef, which are both among the largest fish in the world, may be found all over the planet.

These mysterious creatures have the greatest brain of any fish in the world, and scientists know very little about their behavior and lives. They are also said to be incredibly compassionate creatures. We still have a lot to learn about these amazing animals' intelligence, curiosities, social structures, and interactions, but if we don't act immediately to conserve them, we might not have the chance.

A dilemma is emerging due to the abrupt and recent overfishing of rays. Populations have decreased by as much as 80% in some areas over the past 75 years, and the overall fall is estimated to be approximately 30% globally. To make matters worse, they are particularly susceptible to overfishing and exploitation due to their low birthrate and lengthy growth and maturation cycle.

Rays likewise depend on us to help them live. To end the killing, our laws must be strengthened.
