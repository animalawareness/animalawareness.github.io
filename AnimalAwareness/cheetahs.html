CHEETAHS

Cheetahs are acknowledged as the fastest terrestrial animals in the world due to their long legs and slim bodies. Cheetah populations are currently restricted to the savannahs and grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa, despite once being common throughout Asia and Africa.

Amazingly, cheetahs can sprint over brief distances at speeds of up to 70 mph. They chase down their prey, which mainly comprises of gazelles, wildebeest, and other animals with hooves, by using their speed. The smaller Cheetah does not roar like other big cats do.

Experts in conservation warn that poaching is a major factor in the continued decline of cheetah populations in the wild. In Africa, its natural population has decreased by around 90% since 1980. Only 200 cheetahs survive in the wild in Asia, and they are only found in a few isolated areas of Iran.
