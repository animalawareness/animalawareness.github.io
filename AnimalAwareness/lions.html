LIONS

Lions are well known for their power and majesty. Lions, the only sociable cat species, live in prides made up of females, their cubs, and occasionally a solitary male or a pair or three males. Almost all of the hunting is done by females, who hunt primarily at night. The only larger species of feline aliveÂ after the tiger is the lion. There are numerous risks to lions. As lions have been eradicated from much of Africa, the population of African lions is now less than half of what it was thirty years ago.

The US Fish and Wildlife Service has proposed to list the African Lion as a threatened species under the Endangered Species Act due to poaching by traffickers looking for "alternatives" to endangered tiger products, as well as significant habitat loss and loss of prey base due to human settlement, development, and climate change. Several African nations also allow the legal hunting of lions, which, if done irresponsibly, may affect the population.Although there are still plenty of African lions on the continent, their historical range is thought to have reduced by around 80%, and only ten major strongholds hold about 70% of the present population.

According to studies, African lions may become extinct in the wild by 2050 if nothing is done. We cannot permit that to occur. A significant step toward extending the reign of these kings of the jungle will be continued protection for lions.
