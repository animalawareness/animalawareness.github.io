RHINOCEROS

In the wild, rhinos are getting rarer. In the past, rhinos were once commonly found throughout much of Asia and Africa, but currently just a small percentage of them are still alive. Three rhinos in Africa are thought to be slaughtered on average everyday.

There are five different rhino species: two African (the Southern White and Northern White) and three Asian (the Javan, Sumatran, and Indian). Because of their enormous bodies, short legs, and one or two dermal horns, rhinos, one of the "Big 5" game species, are easily recognized. All five species of rhinos are presently vulnerable, and three of the five are severely endangered (Black, Javan and Sumatran species) due to the demand for these horns as a status symbol, aphrodisiac, and a treatment for cancer in pseudo-traditional Chinese medicine, along with a dramatic loss of habitat. Less than 50 Javan rhinos and 200 Sumatran rhinos are still alive in the wild.  With more than 1,200 murdered in South Africa in 2014, white rhinos are the most frequently poached for the illicit horn trade. Even if several of these species have been saved from extinction by recent conservation efforts, their numbers are still a tiny fraction of what they were fifty years ago.

The desire for rhino horn has remained high and poaching has not decreased despite the restriction on international trading in rhino horn. It is up to us to take action and safeguard these magnificent animals.
