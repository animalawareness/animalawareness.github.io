JAGUARS

The majestic jaguar has a vast geographic range and is the largest wild cat in the Western Hemisphere. After going extinct in 40% of their original habitat, jaguars are now found in 18 countries in Mexico, Central America, and South America. Jaguars are currently under evaluation by the IUCN; if threats from poaching and habitat degradation continue, the species will likely soon be elevated to Vulnerable.

Due to the desire for their paws, teeth, and furs, jaguars are threatened by poaching. In addition, they face threats from prey destruction, retaliatory killings, habitat loss, deforestation, and habitat loss. Since jaguars have already disappeared from their natural habitat in the southwestern United States, we are aware that their populations will continue to decline if nothing is done.
