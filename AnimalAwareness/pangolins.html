PANGOLINS

The most frequently found mammal in Asia's illegal wildlife trade are pangolins, sometimes known as scaly anteaters. They are less well-known than some other endangered species, according to a recent CNN report, which dubbed them "the most trafficked mammal you've never heard of." Alarmingly large numbers of pangolins are killed to satisfy a rising market demand in China.

Pangolins come in eight different species, which are distributed throughout Sub-Saharan Africa and Asia. Pangolins are covered in hard, overlapping scales that resemble plated armor and are sometimes mistaken for reptiles. These creatures, which burrow, use their long, muscular, sticky tongues to consume up to 12,000 ants and termites each day.

Experts in conservation warn that poaching is a major factor in the continued decline of cheetah populations in the wild. In Africa, its natural population has decreased by around 90% since 1980. Only 200 cheetahs survive in the wild in Asia, and they are only found in a few isolated areas of Iran.

Pangolins are hunted for food, for use in traditional medicine, as a source of scales, skins, and flesh for an extensive, illicit international trade. Nearly all pangolin body parts are in high and rising demand, with China having the biggest markets. It is believed that between 117,000 and 235,000 pangolins were killed between 2011 and 2013, and this figure is still rising. What if this amount of slaughter continued unabated for the following ten years? What will remain?

Pangolins are exceptional creatures who depend on human assistance to exist. In order to successfully save these animals, we must enact laws that will actually change the situation.
